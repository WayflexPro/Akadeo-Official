What we're validating

Evidence before headlines.

We're gathering qualitative insight, not vanity metrics. Every pilot cycle ends with interviews, co-planning sessions, and a checklist of improvements before we talk about scale.

Insight-driven decisions

Each prototype is reviewed with educators to confirm it actually reduces prep work or sparks richer discussion. If it doesn't, it ships back to the workshop.

Respect for context

Pilot learnings are shared transparently, along with what still needs refining for diverse classrooms, bandwidth realities, and device limitations.

Responsible rollouts

We will open seats gradually, pairing each new school with a real human on our team. No dark patterns, just steady guidance.

Accessible by design

Every iteration is tested against WCAG targets and low-bandwidth scenarios so the experience remains friendly on older devices.

Building in public

Co-creating with a small circle of educators.

We haven't launched widely yet. Instead, we document every sprint with our pilot partners, publish notes, and invite feedback before promising anything to new schools.

Answers for curious minds.

Is Akadeo suitable for hybrid classrooms?

That's the goal. We're testing features with educators who teach across hybrid settings to ensure the experience feels smooth before we expand access.

How secure is student data?

We're drafting policies to align with FERPA, COPPA, and GDPR requirements. No student data from new schools is stored until those reviews are complete.

Do you offer onboarding support?

Early participants work directly with our team to co-design onboarding. As we learn what works, we'll publish templates that any school can adapt.

Can students access Akadeo on mobile?

Mobile responsiveness is a requirement for launch. We're testing on a range of devices to make sure essential actions work well even on older hardware.

Draft Terms of Service

We're currently preparing our formal terms. The principles below outline how we plan to partner with schools and will be refined in collaboration with legal advisors and early adopters.

Acceptable use

Akadeo is intended for educational communities. We expect every user to respect learners, protect privacy, and use the platform in accordance with applicable laws.

Accounts

Beta access is limited. Administrators are responsible for safeguarding credentials and letting us know immediately if anything looks suspicious.

Liability

While the product is in beta, features may change quickly. We provide Akadeo "as is" and will communicate known risks promptly.

Service levels

Formal uptime guarantees and support SLAs will be shared before general availability. During beta, we announce maintenance windows in advance and respond as quickly as we can.

Contact

Questions? Email legal@akadeo.com. We'll follow up as quickly as possible as we finalize these documents.

Draft Privacy Policy

Transparency matters. This draft will evolve as we complete legal reviews and pilot feedback. No new school data is collected until our policies are finalized.

Information we collect

During beta we gather only the information required to run the pilot (such as tester names and emails) plus anonymous usage analytics to improve the product.

How we use information

Data is used to improve learning workflows, monitor platform health, and provide support. We do not sell personal information and will publish a detailed data map before general availability.

Your choices

You can request access, corrections, or deletion of your information by emailing privacy@akadeo.com. We'll confirm receipt within a few business days.

Data retention

Pilot data is deleted at the end of each testing phase unless you ask us to retain it longer. Final retention controls will be co-designed with districts.

Third-party processors

We are finalizing a list of subprocessors and will share it publicly, along with agreements and change notifications, before onboarding new schools.
